The Story Behind This Room

A Letter From Re

This gathering was born from a gap that no one else was filling. Re shares the story in his own words.

A Letter From Re

A few years ago, something happened that scared me into asking the hardest question: what do I actually want? Not what looks impressive. Not what pays well. What do I want? This gathering is my answer.

I've spent 20 years building brands for other people. I've sat in every mastermind, every high level retreat, every room that promises transformation. And in almost every single one, I was the only gay man at the table.

Not the only one in the building. The only one at the table. The one who had to decide, every single time, how much of himself to bring into the room. The one who could talk about scaling a company but not about what it's like to build a life as a gay man who leads.

I know you know exactly what I'm talking about. Because you've done the same calculation. You've walked into rooms full of brilliant people and still felt like a part of you was sitting in the parking lot.

Higher Love exists because that room, the one where you don't have to leave anything behind, didn't exist. So I built it.

This is not a conference. It's not a networking event. It is a four night, three day gathering for accomplished gay founders and executives at a private luxury venue in the Texas Hill Country. Every conversation, every session, every late night firepit is designed around the topics that sit at the intersection of ambition, identity, and purpose.

The men in this room will be your peers. Not mentors. Not mentees. Equals who understand your world without explanation because they have lived it too.

This gathering is a letter to every 10 year old version of us who had to hide. It says: you get to be whoever you want to be. You get to have everything you want out of life. And you get to belong.

The first people through this door are not filling seats. They are making a statement. And I will remember every single one of them.

If everything on this page resonates, I'd love to talk. Fill out the application and let's find out if this is your room.

With love,
Re Perez

Your Host

Re Perez

I came out in the early nineties in New York City. I was nineteen years old, and back then, it was not safe to come out of the closet. I navigated the streets of New York and the professional work environment while going to NYU, and even though it was a city where you'd think you could be yourself, you still had to be undercover.

Deep down on a personal level, I never really saw myself living past thirty. There weren't a lot of role models of gay men who were vibrant, living full lives past a certain age. And the ones who were didn't feel like me.

It wasn't until I landed in the profession of branding that everything clicked. I approached it from a cultural perspective. How can branding impact culture? How can we use this to influence behavior in a positive way? That lens led me to create a brand culture division for a boutique firm, get recruited by top global agencies, and eventually start my own.

I ran that agency for fourteen years. I helped thousands of clients transform their brands. I wrote the boldly titled bestseller Your Brand Should Be Gay (Even If You're Not). And through all of it, the masterminds, the personal development communities, the meditations, the journeys, I built a reputation as someone who holds a high standard, operates with high integrity, and is masterful at curating rooms and experiences that change people.

But here's the thing. The biggest challenge I found is that I would love to surround myself with other gay men who fit the same vibe. Accomplished, intentional, doing the work, living at a high level. And that room didn't exist.

So I built it.
