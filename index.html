Apply for Your Seat

Hey, brother.

You're here because I personally invited you, or because someone I deeply trust felt this would land.

Let me tell you why.

You've built something extraordinary. A company, a career, a body of work that commands respect. You've sat in boardrooms and spoken on stages. You've navigated masterminds and executive communities with brilliance and composure.

And in almost every one of those rooms, you were the only gay man.

Not because you were hiding. Because no one was building rooms at your level that held all of who you are. You've been invited to a hundred extraordinary tables. Brilliant people, powerful conversations, real access. But none of those tables were set for you. You've always been a welcome guest in someone else's house.

So you code switched. You edited. You brought 90% of yourself and quietly managed the other 10%. You did it so well that most people never noticed.

But you noticed.

And when you do step into gay spaces, the rooms are built for a different season of life, a different level of ambition, or a different kind of man. It's like being fluent in two languages but never finding a room where you can speak both at the same time.

The most accomplished, heart centered gay men in the world have no room that was built for them. They've climbed the mountain the world told them to climb and they're standing at the top asking: "Is this it? Or is there something I haven't been able to access because I've never been in a room where I didn't have to perform?"

I looked for that room for over two decades. I couldn't find it.

So I'm building it.

The most intentional brotherhood ever created for accomplished gay men who've conquered every room except the one built for the whole man.

Your Host

Re Perez

I came out in the early 1990s in New York City at nineteen. Honestly, I never saw myself living past 30. There were no role models of gay men living vibrant, thriving lives past 50. Now at 53, I feel like I could live another 40 years.

For the last 24 years, I've lived at the intersection of two worlds. In one, I'm a brand strategist with a Fortune 500 background who has spent 14 years running Branding for the People. I wrote the bestseller Your Brand Should Be Gay (Even If You're Not) and created the Category of One podcast.

In the other world, I've invested those same years inside the most powerful personal development communities on the planet. Landmark. Tony Robbins. High caliber mastermind retreats. I've hosted transformational retreats, created the Spiritual Capitalism Mastermind, and built a reputation as a superconnector who is masterful at curating rooms and experiences.

I loved both worlds. And in both worlds, I was almost always the only gay man in the room.

Higher Love is what happens when someone finally decides to stop waiting for that room and builds it himself.

The Experience

3.5 Days That Will Change How You
Show Up in Every Room After

A 3.5 day immersive gathering at a private luxury venue in the Texas Hill Country for 22 accomplished gay founders, entrepreneurs, and executives. The room you would have designed yourself if someone handed you a blank canvas and a guest list of the 21 most extraordinary gay men you've never met.

Morning

Guided wellness, meditation, breathwork, and intentional movement in an open air pavilion at sunrise. No performative spirituality. Just the kind of stillness that men like you rarely give themselves permission to access.

Midday

Facilitated peer sessions on the topics that matter most to gay men at your level. Business strategy, legacy, relationships, health, creative expression, and spiritual growth. Imagine a boardroom with the rigor of your best mastermind and the emotional depth of your closest friendship.

Evening

Curated dinners around long tables with candlelight, exceptional food, and the kind of conversation you've been craving. Where you can talk about your exit strategy and your spiritual practice in the same breath and no one flinches.

Late Night

Firepit conversations under the Texas stars. Guards down, armor off, real questions surfacing. When the men around you understand what it cost to build what you've built, those conversations change the trajectory of your next decade.

By the third morning, something will have shifted. You won't be able to name it yet. But you'll notice that you stopped editing yourself sometime on day two, and it didn't cost you anything. In fact, it gave you something back that you forgot you had.
