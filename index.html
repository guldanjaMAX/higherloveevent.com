Qualifying

Is This Your Room?

This is not for the gay man who is still figuring out who he is.

This is for the man who knows, and is ready to stop apologizing for it with his presence.

This room was built for you if

  • You're a gay founder, entrepreneur, or executive who has built something extraordinary, a business, a career, a body of work that reflects who you actually are.
  • You've done the inner work. Landmark. Tony Robbins. Joe Dispenza. Therapy. You've sat with your shadows. You've done the programs, read the books, attended the seminars, and you know that the next layer isn't something you can access alone.
  • You're spiritually curious without being performative about it. You're drawn to consciousness, human design, plant medicine, the bigger questions, but you get quietly frustrated by the spiritual community's tendency toward surface and spiritual bypassing.
  • You're into health and your body. Fitness, optimization, maybe biohacking. The instrument matters to you.
  • You live an intentional life. Your calendar reflects your values, not just your obligations. Who you spend time with is a deliberate choice.
  • You get bored by surface conversation. You are not interested in what the weather is like. You want the conversation that shifts something.
  • You love business but you're building from a different place now, from joy, from purpose, from mission, or you're actively seeking how to make that transition.
  • At times, you don't feel like you fully fit in gay culture. You've always found yourself in rooms that were mostly not gay, not out of shame, but because those were the rooms being built at the level you were operating.
  • Even though you're in your 40s, 50s, maybe early 60s, you still have a lot of life in you. You're on your second or third or fourth mountain. Life is happening through you, not to you.
  • You are ready, genuinely ready, to be in a room where you are not the only one.

Equally Important

This room is not for you if

  • You're coming to network, pitch, or grow your contact list. This room was not designed for surface level exchange.
  • You want the credential without the depth, the presence in the room without the willingness to be present inside it.
  • You're not willing to go deep. Not willing to have vulnerable, uncomfortable conversations.
  • You're not willing to show up fully present.
  • You are not yet ready to let the performance go, even for three and a half days.
