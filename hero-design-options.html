Higher Love Brotherhood — Hero Redesign

Above the Fold

A complete hero redesign inspired by the Maverick1000 site. Full-width background image, actual Higher Love logo (not typed text), bold all-caps centered H1, subheadline, and CTA. The video moves back below the fold to the letter section where it was before. The goal: the user lands and immediately feels the gravity of what this is.

Strategic Analysis

Functional Goal

Immediate identity establishment. Within 2 seconds, the visitor knows: this is a premium brotherhood for entrepreneurial gay men. The background image does the emotional heavy lifting. The H1 does the intellectual framing. The CTA catches anyone ready to act.

The Maverick Pattern

Logo top center. Full-bleed cinematic background image. Large bold centered headline. Italic serif subheadline. One or two CTAs. Scroll cue at bottom. The background image is the soul of the design. It conveys what words cannot: "this is who is in this room."

Background Image Strategy

The Maverick site uses a nature/forest backdrop that conveys "escape from the ordinary." For Higher Love, the image needs to convey brotherhood, warmth, and depth. Options include: men in conversation (silhouettes), an intimate gathering scene, warm architectural interiors, or abstract warmth with golden light.

Video Relocation

The video returns to the letter section below the fold. This follows the Maverick pattern where the hero is purely typographic + imagery. The video becomes a reward for scrolling, not a demand on arrival. This also dramatically simplifies the hero on mobile.

The Gathering
Warmth. Belonging. A room full of light.
91%
Design Direction

Full-viewport hero with a warm, ambient background image of men gathered in golden evening light. The Higher Love stacked logo sits at top center, full SVG, at about 180px wide. Below it, the H1 in Raleway 800 weight, all caps, wide letter-spacing: "THE WORLD'S FIRST BROTHERHOOD FOR ENTREPRENEURIAL GAY MEN." Below the H1, a Cormorant Garamond italic subheadline: "A curated community of growth-oriented gay men in their late 30s, 40s, and 50s." Two CTAs side by side: a solid gold "Request An Invitation" and a ghost-border "Discover Our Story." The entire composition is center-aligned. A subtle 1px gold vertical line descends from the bottom of the hero as a scroll cue. The background has a deep gradient overlay that goes from dark at top and bottom (for logo/text legibility) to slightly lighter in the middle where the image breathes.

Strategic Rationale

This is the direct Maverick translation for Higher Love. The dual-CTA pattern ("Apply" + "Learn More") captures both the ready-to-act visitor and the still-exploring one. The warm gathering background image immediately communicates "these are real men, in a real room, having real connection" without showing specific faces that might alienate. The gradient overlay is critical: it turns any photo into a cinematic moment. The bold uppercase H1 in a sans-serif (Raleway) creates the authoritative, no-apologies energy that Re wants. The italic serif subheadline softens it with specificity.

The Threshold
Bold entry. The headline IS the experience.
88%
Design Direction

Same full-viewport structure, but the typographic hierarchy shifts. The logo is slightly smaller (160px). The H1 is split across two lines where the second line is rendered in gold, creating a visual "reveal" moment: "THE WORLD'S FIRST BROTHERHOOD" in white, then "FOR ENTREPRENEURIAL GAY MEN" in gold. This color shift makes the specificity of the audience impossible to miss — it is not an afterthought, it is the point. The subheadline is in EB Garamond (not italic) for a slightly more grounded, editorial feel. Only one CTA: a solid gold button with dark text ("Request An Invitation") that demands a decision. The background image leans more dramatic — think warm bokeh lights or an evening event atmosphere rather than a literal gathering.

Strategic Rationale

The two-tone headline is a power move. By making "FOR ENTREPRENEURIAL GAY MEN" the gold line, you are saying: this identity is the feature, not the qualifier. The single CTA removes choice paralysis and creates a binary: you are either ready to request an invitation, or you scroll to learn more. No hand-holding. This respects the audience — these are accomplished men who make decisions. The more atmospheric background (lights, bokeh, warm glow) avoids the risk of literal imagery feeling generic, and instead creates an emotional texture that says "something special is happening here."

The Ember
Intimate. Grounded. The warmth before the fire.
82%
Design Direction

The most understated of the three. The logo is larger (200px) and given more vertical breathing room. Below it, a thin gold gradient divider. The H1 is bold but slightly smaller than the other options, letting the words sit with more dignity on the page. Below the H1, another thin gold divider, then the subheadline in larger Cormorant Garamond italic — almost a pull quote. Two CTAs: a soft gold fill button and a text-link style "Learn Our Story" with a bottom border. The background image is the most intimate: think friends in deep conversation, or a close-up of hands holding whiskey glasses, or a table set for a meaningful dinner. The overlay is heavier, making the image feel like a memory or a mood rather than a literal scene. A subtle radial gold glow sits behind the center content.

Strategic Rationale

Where the other two concepts lead with authority, this one leads with intimacy. The larger logo and additional breathing room say "we do not need to shout." The dividers between elements create a sense of ceremony — this is not a landing page, this is an invitation. The risk is that it may feel too quiet for a first-time visitor who needs to be grabbed. But for the man who already feels something pulling him toward this, the restraint is the signal that this is real.

Background Image Directions

Six directions for the hero background, from literal to abstract. Each can be sourced from stock, shot custom, or generated with AI.

The Brotherhood Moment

Accomplished men in their 40s and 50s sharing a real moment. Not posed, not performing. The Integrated King in his natural element: present, grounded, connected. Think retreat setting, fireside, or a meaningful meal together. Dark-toned with golden hour light. This is the most direct "show what the room feels like" approach.

Recommended

The Evening Atmosphere

Warm golden light, no faces visible. The feeling of walking into a room where something meaningful is about to happen. Amber tones, soft focus. This lets the typography carry the identity while the image carries the emotion. No alcohol, no party energy. Just warmth and presence.

Recommended

The Golden Blur

Abstract golden warmth. No people, no specific setting. Pure emotional texture: warmth, depth, presence. The safest option because it avoids stock-photo pitfalls entirely and lets the bold typography do the work. Every overlay treatment works with this.

Strong Alternative

The Room Itself

A warm, intimate gathering space. Think: a lodge with firelight, a private retreat room, a long table set for brothers. The literal "room" metaphor from the copy. Not corporate, not a hotel ballroom. Something that feels handpicked and intentional, like the brotherhood itself.

Strong Alternative

The Austin Horizon

Texas Hill Country or open horizon at golden hour. Expansiveness, possibility, warmth. Ties the brand to its Austin home while conveying "something bigger than yourself." Similar to how Maverick uses Pacific Northwest nature. No people needed. The landscape IS the metaphor.

Worth Exploring

AI Generated: Brotherhood Silhouettes

Use Imagen 4 to generate the exact scene: a circle of distinguished men in their 40s and 50s, gathered around a fire or long table, shot from behind or at a distance. Warm amber tones. No alcohol. The mood is presence, not performance. This gives you an image tailored exactly to the Integrated King avatar that no stock library can provide.

Which Direction?

The Gathering (91%) is the strongest option. It is the closest to the Maverick reference Re loved, with the dual-CTA pattern that captures both ready and exploring visitors. The warm gathering background makes the brand feel real and aspirational. It is the safe bet that will definitely work.

The Threshold (88%) is the boldest option. The two-tone headline where "FOR ENTREPRENEURIAL GAY MEN" renders in gold is a powerful design choice that centers the audience's identity as the feature, not the qualifier. Single CTA means it requires more conviction but rewards it with clarity. If Re wants to make the gayness feel unapologetic and celebrated, this is the one.

The Ember (82%) is the most refined option. More breathing room, more ceremony, more restraint. It is beautiful but risks being too quiet for a first impression. Best suited if the brand leans more toward private luxury than community energy.

For the background image, I would recommend starting with "The Evening Atmosphere" (warm bokeh/event lighting) or commissioning an AI-generated "Brotherhood Silhouettes" scene. Both avoid the pitfall of stock photos looking generic while carrying the exact emotional tone this brand needs.
