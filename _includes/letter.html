Your Host

Re Perez

I came out in the early nineties in New York City. I was nineteen years old, and back then, it was not safe to come out of the closet. I navigated the streets of New York and the professional work environment while going to NYU, and even though it was a city where you would think you could be yourself, you still had to be undercover.

Deep down on a personal level, I never really saw myself living past thirty. There were not a lot of role models of gay men who were vibrant, living full lives past a certain age. And the ones who were did not feel like me.

It was not until I landed in the profession of branding that everything clicked. I approached it from a cultural perspective. How can branding impact culture? How can we use this to influence behavior in a positive way? That lens led me to create a brand culture division for a boutique firm, get recruited by top global agencies, and eventually start my own.

I ran that agency for fourteen years. I helped thousands of clients transform their brands. I wrote the boldly titled bestseller Your Brand Should Be Gay (Even If You’re Not). And through all of it, the masterminds, the personal development communities, the meditations, the journeys, I built a reputation as someone who holds a high standard, operates with high integrity, and is masterful at curating rooms and experiences that change people.

I sat in every mastermind, every high level retreat, every room that promises transformation. I was respected. I was welcomed. And in almost every single one, I was the only gay man at the table. Not the only one in the building. The only one at the table. The one who had to decide, every single time, how much of himself to bring into the room.

Last year, at 52 years old, I was diagnosed with prostate cancer. When something like that happens, it does not ask permission. It sits you down and strips everything away until all that is left are the questions that actually matter.

Are you living fully? Or are you living carefully?

I sat with those questions. And I realized the answer was yes. But a 70% yes.

I had spent 20 years building brands for other people. I had been respected, welcomed, and invited into every room imaginable. And in almost every single one, I was the one who could talk about scaling a company but not about what it is like to build a life as a gay man who leads.

You have built something remarkable. And you still feel lonely. I know, because I felt it too. That specific loneliness of being surrounded by brilliant people and still not being fully known.

I know you know exactly what I am talking about. Because you have done the same calculation. You have walked into rooms full of brilliant people and still felt like a part of you was sitting in the parking lot.

If I could write a letter to my 19 year old self, the one who came out in 1990s New York and navigated a world that told him to be invisible, I would say this:

You get to be whoever you want to be. You get to have everything you want out of life. And you get to belong. Not despite who you are. Because of who you are.

This gathering is that letter, written for every version of us who had to hide.

I am done with 70%. I am not getting old. I am just getting started.

The first people through this door are not filling seats. They are making a statement. And I will remember every single one of them.

If everything on this page resonates, I would love to talk. Fill out the application and let us find out if this is your room.

The room I was looking for did not exist. So I built it.
