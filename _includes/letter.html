A Letter From Re

Last year, at 52 years old, I was diagnosed with prostate cancer. When something like that happens, it does not ask permission. It sits you down and strips everything away until all that is left are the questions that actually matter.

Are you living fully? Or are you living carefully?

I sat with those questions. And I realized the answer was yes. But a 70% yes.

I had spent 20 years building brands for other people. I sat in every mastermind, every high level retreat, every room that promises transformation. I was respected. I was welcomed. And in almost every single one, I was the only gay man at the table.

Not the only one in the building. The only one at the table. The one who had to decide, every single time, how much of himself to bring into the room. The one who could talk about scaling a company but not about what it is like to build a life as a gay man who leads.

You have built something remarkable. And you still feel lonely. I know, because I felt it too. That specific loneliness of being surrounded by brilliant people and still not being fully known.

I know you know exactly what I am talking about. Because you have done the same calculation. You have walked into rooms full of brilliant people and still felt like a part of you was sitting in the parking lot.

Higher Love exists because that room, the one where you do not have to leave anything behind, did not exist. So I built it.

This is not a conference. It is not a networking event. It is a four night, three day gathering for accomplished gay founders and executives at a private luxury venue in Austin, Texas. Every conversation, every session, every late night firepit is designed around the topics that sit at the intersection of ambition, identity, and purpose.

The men in this room will be your peers. Not mentors. Not mentees. Equals who understand your world without explanation because they have lived it too.

If I could write a letter to my 19 year old self, the one who came out in 1990s New York and navigated a world that told him to be invisible, I would say this:

You get to be whoever you want to be. You get to have everything you want out of life. And you get to belong. Not despite who you are. Because of who you are.

This gathering is that letter, written for every version of us who had to hide.

I am done with 70%. I am not getting old. I am just getting started.

The first people through this door are not filling seats. They are making a statement. And I will remember every single one of them.

If everything on this page resonates, I would love to talk. Fill out the application and let us find out if this is your room.

With love,
Re Perez
