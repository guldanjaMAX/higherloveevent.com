Your Host

Re Perez

I came out in the early nineties in New York City. I was nineteen years old, and back then, it was not safe to come out of the closet. I navigated the streets of New York and the professional work environment while going to NYU, and even though it was a city where you'd think you could be yourself, you still had to be undercover.

Deep down on a personal level, I never really saw myself living past thirty. There weren't a lot of role models of gay men who were vibrant, living full lives past a certain age. And the ones who were didn't feel like me.

It wasn't until I landed in the profession of branding that everything clicked. I approached it from a cultural perspective. How can branding impact culture? How can we use this to influence behavior in a positive way? That lens led me to create a brand culture division for a boutique firm, get recruited by top global agencies, and eventually start my own.

I ran that agency for fourteen years. I helped thousands of clients transform their brands. I wrote the boldly titled bestseller Your Brand Should Be Gay (Even If You're Not). And through all of it, the masterminds, the personal development communities, the meditations, the journeys, I built a reputation as someone who holds a high standard, operates with high integrity, and is masterful at curating rooms and experiences that change people.

But here's the thing. The biggest challenge I found is that I would love to surround myself with other gay men who fit the same vibe. Accomplished, intentional, doing the work, living at a high level. And that room didn't exist.

So I built it.

The Experience

Three Days.
One Room That Changes Everything.

By the second day, something shifts. You stop editing yourself. You stop scanning the room. You just... exhale. That is what three days in this room does. A private luxury venue in the Austin, Texas, September 18–20, 2026. Every conversation, every late night firepit, every quiet morning is designed around what actually matters to you.

By the third morning, something will have shifted. You won't be able to name it yet. But you'll notice that you stopped editing yourself sometime on day two, and it didn't cost you anything. In fact, it gave you something back that you forgot you had.
